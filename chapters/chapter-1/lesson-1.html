What is Chemistry?

Approach: Real-life example first, then theory

🪝 Hook

Right now, take a quick look around your room. You see:

  • The air you are breathing
  • A glass of water maybe nearby
  • Your phone screen made of glass and plastic
  • The chai your family drinks
  • Salt in the kitchen
  • Petrol in motorbikes outside

Here's the magical truth: Everything you just looked at is made of tiny invisible particles, and the study of these particles — what they are, how they behave, and how they change — is called Chemistry.

Even you are a walking chemistry experiment. Your body is doing thousands of chemical reactions every second right now! 🤯

📘 Core Concept

Textbook Definition

Chemistry is the branch of science that deals with the composition, structure, properties, and changes of matter.

My Easy Version

Chemistry is the science of "what things are made of" and "how they change into other things."

Breakdown table

Word What it means Example
Composition What is it made of? Water is made of hydrogen + oxygen
Structure How are the pieces arranged? Diamond and pencil lead are both carbon, but arranged differently
Properties How does it behave? Salt dissolves in water, oil does not
Changes How does it turn into something else? Wood burning → ash + smoke

What is matter?

Matter = anything that has mass AND takes up space.

        MATTER
          |
    ┌─────┴─────┐
    |           |
  Has mass    Takes space
  (weight)    (volume)

Test examples:

  • Air → has mass + takes space → ✅ Matter
  • A thought → no mass, no space → ❌ Not matter
  • Light → no mass → ❌ Not matter (energy, not matter)
  • School bag → ✅ Matter
  • Water → ✅ Matter

Branches of chemistry

Branch What it studies Real-life example
Physical Chemistry Physics + Chemistry combined (energy, speed of reactions) Why ice melts faster in summer
Organic Chemistry Carbon-based compounds (living things, fuels) Petrol, plastic, sugar
Inorganic Chemistry Everything that is NOT carbon-based Salt, metals, water
Biochemistry Chemistry inside living bodies How your body digests biryani
Analytical Chemistry Finding what a substance is made of Lab testing your blood report
Nuclear Chemistry Chemistry of the nucleus (radioactivity) Nuclear power plants, X-rays
Industrial Chemistry Making products on a large scale Cement, soap, fertilizer factories

🌍 Real-Life Examples

  1. Cooking 🍳 — When you fry an egg, the clear part turns white. That's a chemical change!
  2. Rusting 🔩 — Iron gate turning brown over time = chemistry
  3. Photosynthesis 🌱 — Plants making food from sunlight = chemistry
  4. Medicine 💊 — Panadol curing your headache = chemistry
  5. Battery 🔋 — Your phone working = chemistry inside the battery
  6. Soap 🧼 — Removes oil from your hands = chemistry

⚠️ Common Confusions

Confusion #1: "Is air really matter? It feels like nothing!"

✅ Yes! Air has mass. Take an empty balloon, weigh it. Now fill it with air and weigh again. The filled one is heavier.

Confusion #2: "Chemistry = just memorizing formulas"

❌ Wrong! Chemistry is mostly understanding. Once you understand why things happen, formulas become easy.

Confusion #3: "Organic = healthy/natural food (like organic vegetables)"

❌ In chemistry, "organic" means containing carbon. It has nothing to do with farming or health food!

Bonus — Why light is not matter

Light is made of photons which have zero rest mass. It is a form of energy, not matter.

Property Stone 🪨 Light 💡
Can you weigh it?✅ Yes❌ No
Does it take space?✅ Yes❌ No
Has mass?✅ Yes❌ No
Is it matter?✅ YES❌ NO

Universe = Matter (has mass: stone, water, air, you) + Energy (no mass: light, heat, sound, electricity)

Short Questions

Q4. Define matter and give two examples.

Answer: Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. Examples: water, air, your school bag, stone.

Q5. Name any three branches of chemistry and write one line about each.

Sample answer: Organic chemistry (carbon compounds, e.g. petrol); Inorganic chemistry (non-carbon compounds, e.g. salt); Biochemistry (chemistry in living things, e.g. digestion).

📋 Summary

  1. Chemistry = study of what things are made of and how they change
  2. Matter = anything with mass + volume (everything physical around you)
  3. 7 main branches of chemistry, each studying a different area
