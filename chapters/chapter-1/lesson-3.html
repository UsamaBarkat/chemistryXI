Chapter 1 → Topic 3 of 7

Elements, Compounds, and Mixtures

Approach: Real-life examples first, then theory

🪝 Hook

Look at these three things on a kitchen shelf:

  • A piece of pure gold — same all the way through, cannot be broken down
  • A spoon of salt (NaCl) — two different atoms joined together chemically
  • A glass of namkeen lassi — salt + yogurt + water, just mixed, not joined

These three represent the three types of all matter in the universe: Element, Compound, and Mixture. By the end of this lesson you will be able to tell which is which — instantly.

📘 Core Concept

1. Element

Textbook Definition

An element is a pure substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by any chemical or physical method.

My Easy Version

An element is the simplest form of matter. It is made of only ONE type of atom. You cannot split it further.

There are 118 elements known so far — all listed in the Periodic Table.

2. Compound

Textbook Definition

A compound is a pure substance formed when two or more elements are chemically combined together in a fixed ratio.

My Easy Version

A compound is when two or more elements are joined together so tightly that you need a chemical reaction to separate them. They always join in the same fixed amounts.

CompoundElements insideReal-life use
Water (H₂O)Hydrogen + OxygenDrinking, cooking
Salt (NaCl)Sodium + ChlorineFood seasoning
Sugar (C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁)Carbon + Hydrogen + OxygenChai, sweets

Key point for exam: In a compound, the elements combine in a fixed ratio. Water is always H₂O — never H₃O or HO. That ratio never changes.

3. Mixture

Textbook Definition

A mixture is a combination of two or more substances (elements or compounds) that are NOT chemically combined. Each substance keeps its own properties and can be separated by physical methods.

My Easy Version

A mixture is when you just put things together without any chemical reaction. Like daal and rice on a plate — they are together but not joined. You can pick them apart.

🌍 Real-Life Examples

  1. Gold jewellery 💍 — Pure gold is an element. But 22-carat gold is a mixture (gold + copper).
  2. Table salt 🧂 — NaCl is a compound. Sodium alone explodes in water. Chlorine alone is a poisonous gas. But together they make safe eating salt!
  3. Air 🌬️ — Air is a mixture. It is mostly nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%). You can separate them — that is how hospitals get pure oxygen.
  4. Chai ☕ — Tea is a mixture of water, milk, sugar, tea leaves — all mixed but not chemically joined.
  5. Rust 🔩 — Rust (Fe₂O₃) is a compound formed when iron and oxygen combine chemically. You cannot just "unmix" them physically.

⚠️ Common Confusions

Confusion #1: "Air is a compound because it has many gases combined"

❌ Wrong! In air, gases are just mixed — not chemically joined. The ratio of gases in air can vary from place to place. Compounds always have a fixed ratio. So air is a mixture.

Confusion #2: "Water is a mixture of hydrogen and oxygen"

❌ Wrong! Hydrogen and oxygen are chemically bonded in water (H₂O) in a fixed 2:1 ratio. You cannot separate them by filtering or boiling. Water is a compound.

Confusion #3: "Salt water is a compound"

❌ Wrong! You can get the salt back just by evaporating the water. No chemical reaction needed. So salt water is a mixture (homogeneous).

Confusion #4: "A compound is the same as a mixture"

❌ Big mistake! In a compound, elements lose their own identity and form something completely new. Sodium (dangerous metal) + Chlorine (poisonous gas) → Salt (safe to eat). In a mixture, everything keeps its own identity.

Short Questions

Q9. Define element and give two examples.

Answer: An element is a pure substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by any chemical method. It contains only one type of atom. Examples: Iron (Fe), Oxygen (O).

Q10. What is the difference between a compound and a mixture? Give one example of each.

Answer: In a compound, elements are chemically combined in a fixed ratio and cannot be separated by physical methods — e.g. Water (H₂O). In a mixture, substances are physically combined in any ratio and can be separated by physical methods — e.g. Salt water.

Q11. Classify the following as element, compound, or mixture: (i) Gold (ii) CO₂ (iii) Muddy water

Answer: (i) Gold → Element   (ii) CO₂ → Compound   (iii) Muddy water → Mixture (heterogeneous)

📋 Summary

  1. Element — one type of atom, cannot be broken down chemically (e.g. Fe, O, Cu)
  2. Compound — two or more elements joined chemically in a fixed ratio; has new properties (e.g. H₂O, NaCl)
  3. Mixture — two or more substances physically mixed; no fixed ratio; each keeps its properties (e.g. air, salt water)
  4. Homogeneous mixture — uniform throughout (e.g. sugar in chai)
  5. Heterogeneous mixture — non-uniform; parts visible (e.g. muddy water)
  6. Mixtures are separated by physical methods; compounds by chemical reactions
